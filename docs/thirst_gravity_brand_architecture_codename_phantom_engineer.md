# Thirst‑Gravity Brand Architecture — Codename: PHANTOM ENGINEER

**Guiding Line:** Every frame a weapon. Every post an operation.

---

## 0) Executive Thesis
**Thirst** creates pull (desire, intrigue, aesthetic hunger). **Gravity** creates orbit (rituals, retention, relationships). Brand wins when beauty, evidence, and repeatable systems make people want more **and** give them a reason to stay.

**Brand Promise (fill):** _We deliver {specific transformation/outcome} with cinematic precision and measurable results._

**Proof Modes:** visual proof in first frame, outcome receipts, repeatable formats, community artifacts.

## 5) Visual Language & Motion Grammar
**Palette (HEX):** #0D0D0F (noir), #C9B27E (champagne), #E8E1D4 (ivory), #7A7A7A (steel).

**Type:** Grotesk primary (clean, geometric), Serif accent for codes/quotes.

**Lens/Lighting Presets:**
- **Champagne Rim:** warm back rim + soft key; 50mm f/1.8; subtle bloom; Kodak 2383 curve.
- **Silky Noir:** edge light + negative fill; 85mm; fine grain.

**Shot Catalog:** tight medium, hand micro‑motions, 2% dolly‑in; first/last frame mirror for loops.

**Edit Pacing:** new micro‑idea every 2–3s; macro beat ≤ 40s; SFX punctuate state changes.

## 6) Thirst‑Gravity Mechanics
**Thirst (Pull):**

## 7) Content Pillars → Formats
**P1. Systems & Proof** — teardown, audits, before/after.
- Formats: 9–18s “Cold Proof,” 30–60s “Blueprint to Payoff.”

**P2. Myth & Aesthetic** — cinematic micro‑rituals, object altars.
- Formats: 7–12s loop with mirror frame; OST sting.

**P3. Ops & Tutorials** — beatmaps, checklists, live fixes.
- Formats: 20–40s step stacks; over‑shoulder screen.

**P4. Community & Lore** — Q&A, artifact unboxing, patch ceremonies.
- Formats: 15–30s response chains; “Patch Notes.”

## 8) Packaging System (Titles, Covers, Captions)
**Title Patterns:**
- “Declassifying the {Result} Blueprint.”
- “We rebuilt {X} without {common crutch}.”
- “I audit your channel in 60 seconds. Watch.”

**Cover Frames:** proof object centered; blueprint grid; Ops ID; minimal text upper‑left.

**Caption Grammar:** lead with time‑trap → one insight → save/share command.
- Example: “Watch {0:07} for the pivot. Save to rebuild your hook library.”

**CTA Rails:** Save/Share > Comment > Follow; “Deploy this; report back with your SpV.”

---

## 9) Platform Doctrine & Metric Guardrails
**Reels/TikTok/Shorts:** first‑second novelty; payoff by 3–6s.
- Track: 3‑second hold % (target ≥ 65%), Completion % (Shorts ≥ 40%), Saves per View (SpV ≥ 1.5%).

**YouTube Long:** thumbnail/title proof, major beat ≤ 40s, end‑screen loops.
- Track: CTR (topic‑norm +2–3pp), AVD, session starts, end‑screen CTR.

**Instagram Grid:** grid coherence; Saves/Likes ratio target ≥ 0.6; cover discipline.

**X/Twitter:** dwell + replies; link in follow‑up; quote‑fuel prompts.

---

## 10) Measurement & Scoring
**Virality Score (working):** (Emotional Impact + Uniqueness + Trend Alignment) / (Relatability + Clarity + Shareability) × 10.

**Instrumentation:**
- Manual: retention graphs, save counts, comment heatmaps.
- Auto: template spreadsheet; clip‑ID, hook type, payoff time, SpV, rerun score.

**Cadence Review:** daily 10‑minute after‑action; weekly cull of losers; archive protects signal.

---

## 11) Ethics & Guardrails
- No cloning real people; fictional or consenting models only.
- PG‑13 boundaries; no explicit content; avoid risky tokens (use safer synonyms).
- Platform ToS aligned; watermarking accepted when applied by platform.
- Transparent claims; receipts over rhetoric.
